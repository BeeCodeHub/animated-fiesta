About me

Professor Florian Luca

I am a mathematician from Romania who focuses on number theory, particularly Diophantine equations, linear recurrences, and the distribution of arithmetic function values. I have contributed to proving that irrational automatic numbers are transcendental and to proving Erdős' conjecture on the intersection of the Euler Totient function and the sum of divisors function. I completed my undergraduate studies in Mathematics at Alexandru Ioan Cuza University in Iași in 1992, and then went on to earn a Ph.D. in Mathematics from the University of Alaska Fairbanks in 1996. I have been assigned different positions at Syracuse University, Bielefeld University, Czech Academy of Sciences, National Autonomous University of Mexico and University of Witwatersrand. At the moment, I hold the position of research professor at the Stellenbosch University. I have collaborated with over 200 co-authors on more than 500 mathematics papers. I received the 2005 Guggenheim Fellowship for Natural Sciences, Latin America & Caribbean.
